Chole Bhature

Description

Chole Bhature is a classic and popular Punjabi meal of a spicy, tangy chickpea curry (chole) paired with a soft, fluffy, deep-fried leavened bread (bhature). This hearty and indulgent combination of Chole Bhature is often enjoyed with pickles, onions, and lemon and is a popular choice for breakfast or brunch across North India.

Ingredients required

  • Chickpeas (soaked overnight)
  • Spices
  • onions
  • tomatoes
  • flavour enhancers
  • Flour

Preparation steps

  1. Chole Preparation
    1. rinse 1 cup white chickpeas a couple of times in water. Then soak them in enough water to cover them for 7-8 hours or overnight.
    2. Drain the water and rinse the chickpeas with fresh water a few times the next day
    3. Add the soaked chickpeas to a 3-liter stovetop pressure cooker. Also add 3 cups water and ¼ teaspoon salt.
    4. Pressure cook chickpeas for 10 to 12 whistles (15 to 20 minutes), or till they are tender and cooked well. Allow the pressure to release naturally, then check the consistency. It is important to cook the chickpeas till they are melt-in-the-mouth soft.
    5. Once cooked well, drain all the water and set the cooked chickpeas aside.
  2. Making onion tomato masala
    1. Chop 1 medium-sized onion, 2 medium-sized tomatoes, 1 green chili, 4 to 5 medium-sized garlic cloves and 1 inch ginger.
    2. Add them to a blender jar
    3. Make a fine paste in a grinder or blender, then set aside.
  3. Making chickpea gravy (Chole)
    1. Measure out all the ingredients for making the gravy before you start.
    2. Heat 2 to 3 tablespoons oil in a pan or kadai/wok. Add the following whole garam masala (spices):
      • 1 tbsp cumin seeds
      • 1 large Indian bay leaf
      • 2 inch cinnamon stick
      • 2 to 3 cloves
      • 2 green cardamom
      • 2 to 3 black peppercorns
    3. Fry the whole spices till they are fragrant but don't burn them.
    4. Next, add the ground masala paste and mix well.
    5. Keep stirring often. Sauté for 8 to 10 minutes (or more), till the oil starts to leave the sides of the masala paste. The paste will also thicken and become glossy.
    6. Serve chana curry with onions, wedges and bhature for the best taste.
